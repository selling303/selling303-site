<p>If you're a <a href="/first-time-homebuyers">first-time home buyer</a> scanning the South Denver Metro suburbs and thinking you can't afford anything — pause. <a href="/neighborhoods/englewood">Englewood</a> is the entry point most buyers overlook, and that's exactly why it deserves a closer look in 2026.</p>

<p>While Highlands Ranch and Parker get most of the attention from families, Englewood consistently offers lower price-per-square-foot, better transit connectivity, and the kind of walkable neighborhood feel that's hard to find in newer suburban developments. For buyers working with a tighter budget — or anyone who wants to stop renting without moving 30 miles from downtown Denver — Englewood belongs on the list.</p>

<p>This guide breaks down pricing, financing options, neighborhoods, commute, and lifestyle — everything a first-time buyer needs to evaluate whether Englewood, Colorado fits the plan.</p>

<h2 id="why-englewood">Why Englewood Works for First-Time Buyers</h2>

<p>Englewood sits just south of Denver proper, tucked between the South Platte River and the western edge of Centennial. It's one of the older, more established communities in Arapahoe County — which is exactly what makes it affordable. You're not paying a premium for new construction finishes or HOA-managed landscaping. You're getting solid mid-century housing stock, mature trees, and a street grid that was built before every neighborhood needed a gate.</p>

<p>For first-time buyers, the math works here in ways it doesn't in most South Denver Metro suburbs. The median home price in Englewood runs well below the broader <a href="https://www.redfin.com/city/30945/CO/Denver/housing-market" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Denver Metro median</a>, which means lower down payments, lower monthly payments, and a realistic path to ownership on a single income or a dual-income household that isn't pulling six figures.</p>

<p>There's also a practical advantage: Englewood's housing mix includes condos, townhomes, and single-family homes across a wide price range. First-time buyers aren't boxed into one product type the way they might be in a master-planned community where everything starts at $550K.</p>

<h2 id="pricing">Entry-Level Pricing and What to Expect</h2>

<p>Englewood's entry-level market breaks into three tiers that matter for first-time buyers:</p>

<p><strong>Condos and townhomes ($280K–$380K):</strong> This is the lowest entry point in the South Denver Metro. One- and two-bedroom condos near CityCenter Englewood, along South Broadway, or in the complexes south of Hampden Avenue regularly list in this range. These are realistic targets for buyers putting 3.5% down with an FHA loan.</p>

<p><strong>Starter single-family homes ($400K–$500K):</strong> Two- and three-bedroom ranch-style homes — 1950s to 1970s builds — with 900 to 1,400 square feet. Many need cosmetic updates, which keeps prices accessible. These are the bread-and-butter listings for Englewood first-time buyers.</p>

<p><strong>Updated or larger homes ($500K–$600K):</strong> Renovated ranches, split-levels, and the occasional mid-century gem with a finished basement. This tier competes with entry-level pricing in Centennial and Littleton, but you typically get more lot size for the money in Englewood.</p>

<p>One thing to watch: Englewood's proximity to Denver and the continued development around CityCenter and <a href="https://www.englewoodco.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the City of Englewood's</a> redevelopment zones means pricing has been trending upward. Buying now, while the gap between Englewood and neighboring suburbs is still significant, is the move.</p>

<h2 id="financing">FHA, Conventional, and Down Payment Options</h2>

<p>Most first-time buyers in Englewood are choosing between two loan types — and depending on your financial situation, either one could be a fit. Be sure to consult a mortgage professional to determine which option works best for you.</p>

<h3>FHA Loans (3.5% Down)</h3>

<p>The 2026 FHA loan limit for Arapahoe County allows most Englewood single-family homes to qualify. At 3.5% down on a $430K home, your down payment lands around $15,050 — a number that's actually reachable with disciplined saving or down payment assistance programs. The trade-off is mortgage insurance premiums (MIP), which add to your monthly payment for the life of the loan unless you refinance later.</p>

<p>For condos, there's an extra step: the complex needs to be on the <a href="https://entp.hud.gov/idapp/html/condlook.cfm" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FHA-approved condo list</a>. Not all Englewood complexes are, so check before you fall in love with a unit.</p>

<h3>Conventional Loans (3–5% Down)</h3>

<p>If your credit score is 700+, a conventional loan with 3–5% down often makes more sense. You'll avoid the upfront MIP, and the private mortgage insurance (PMI) drops off once you hit 20% equity — unlike FHA's permanent MIP. For a first-time buyer in Englewood planning to build equity and either stay or sell up in 5–7 years, conventional financing gives you a cleaner exit.</p>

<h3>Down Payment Assistance</h3>

<p>Colorado offers several down payment assistance programs through <a href="https://www.chfainfo.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">CHFA (Colorado Housing and Finance Authority)</a> that first-time buyers in Englewood should explore. These programs can cover part or all of the down payment as a grant or forgivable loan. Eligibility depends on income limits and purchase price caps — but Englewood's lower price points often fall within the qualifying range.</p>

<h2 id="neighborhoods">Englewood Neighborhoods to Watch</h2>

<p>Englewood is compact — about 6.5 square miles — but the character shifts noticeably from one pocket to another. Here's where first-time buyers should focus:</p>

<h3>CityCenter / Downtown Englewood</h3>

<p>The area around CityCenter Englewood (the redeveloped Cinderella City site) is Englewood's most walkable zone. Light rail access, restaurants along South Broadway, the Englewood Public Library, and the Englewood Civic Center are all within a few blocks. Condos and townhomes here price at the lower end of the market, and the area is actively redeveloping — which means upside for early buyers.</p>

<h3>South Broadway Corridor</h3>

<p>South Broadway through Englewood has evolved into one of the metro's best small-business strips. Antique shops, breweries, restaurants, and locally owned retail give this area a distinctly non-suburban feel. Housing along the corridor is a mix of older bungalows and small-lot homes. If walkability to dining and nightlife matters more than a big backyard, this is your neighborhood.</p>

<h3>Bates / Logan / Grant Avenues (East Englewood)</h3>

<p>The residential streets east of Broadway and south of Hampden are classic Englewood — tree-lined blocks with 1950s ranches, quiet streets, and a neighborhood-park-on-every-corner feel. This area is where the $400K–$500K starter homes tend to cluster. It's less walkable to commercial areas but offers more space and a calmer pace.</p>

<h3>West of South Santa Fe</h3>

<p>The neighborhoods west of Santa Fe Drive sit closer to the South Platte River trail system and tend to be slightly more affordable. You'll find smaller lots and older homes, but the access to the river trail, Mary Carter Greenway, and open space is a real lifestyle draw for buyers who prioritize outdoor access.</p>

<h2 id="walkability">Walkability, Transit, and Commute</h2>

<p>This is where Englewood punches above its weight compared to other South Denver Metro suburbs.</p>

<p><strong>RTD Light Rail:</strong> The Englewood Station on the E, F, and H lines puts downtown Denver about 20 minutes away without touching I-25. For first-time buyers who work in the Denver Tech Center, downtown, or anywhere along the light rail corridor, this is a major quality-of-life advantage. No other South Denver suburb in the $400K price range offers comparable transit access.</p>

<p><strong>Walkability:</strong> Englewood's older street grid — short blocks, sidewalks, connected streets — creates a walkability score that most master-planned communities can't touch. You can walk to a grocery store, a coffee shop, and a park from most Englewood neighborhoods. That's not always true in Highlands Ranch or Parker.</p>

<p><strong>Commute by car:</strong> Englewood sits at the intersection of US-285, Santa Fe Drive, and Broadway, with quick access to I-25. Commutes to the DTC run 15–20 minutes. Downtown Denver is 15 minutes outside of rush hour. The trade-off: I-25 northbound in the morning is brutal, just like everywhere else on the Front Range.</p>

<h2 id="lifestyle">Lifestyle Fit and Local Amenities</h2>

<p>Englewood attracts a specific kind of first-time buyer — someone who values character over newness, convenience over square footage, and proximity over prestige. It's not for everyone, and that's fine. Here's who it fits best:</p>

<p><strong>Young professionals:</strong> The South Broadway scene, light rail commute, and lower price points make Englewood a natural landing spot for buyers in their late 20s and 30s who are done renting in Denver but don't want to move to a subdivision 25 miles out.</p>

<p><strong>Remote and hybrid workers:</strong> If your commute is three days a week or less, Englewood's lower housing costs give you more financial breathing room than buying in Denver proper — without sacrificing the urban amenities you'd miss in a more suburban setting.</p>

<p><strong>Outdoor-oriented buyers:</strong> The South Platte River Trail runs through Englewood's western edge, connecting to the Mary Carter Greenway and Chatfield State Park to the south. Pirates Cove Water Park, Belleview Park, and Broken Tee Golf Course are all within city limits. For an inner-ring suburb, the outdoor access is unusually strong.</p>

<p>Dining and entertainment lean local and independent — South Broadway's stretch through Englewood is one of the best small-business corridors in the metro. The Streets at SouthGlenn (technically in Centennial, but bordering Englewood) adds a more traditional retail and restaurant hub.</p>

<h2 id="mistakes">Common First-Time Buyer Mistakes in Englewood</h2>

<p>Jacob Stark works with first-time buyers in Englewood regularly, and a few patterns come up consistently:</p>

<p><strong>Skipping the pre-approval:</strong> In a market where Englewood homes under $450K move fast, showing up without a pre-approval letter means losing to buyers who are already qualified. Get pre-approved before you start touring — not after you find something you love.</p>

<p><strong>Overlooking older home costs:</strong> Englewood's housing stock is predominantly mid-century. That means potential costs for updated electrical panels, sewer line scoping (older clay pipes), and HVAC replacement. A thorough home inspection isn't optional here — it's the most important money you'll spend in the transaction. Budget for it, and don't waive it to compete.</p>

<p><strong>Ignoring the condo HOA deep-dive:</strong> If you're purchasing an Englewood condo, review the HOA financials carefully. Ask for the reserve study, monthly dues history, and any upcoming special assessments. A $290K condo with a $500/month HOA and a pending $10K special assessment isn't the deal it looks like on Zillow.</p>

<p><strong>Waiting for the "perfect" market:</strong> First-time buyers in Englewood often hesitate, waiting for rates to drop or prices to dip. Meanwhile, they're paying rent that builds zero equity. The math almost always favors buying sooner in a market where appreciation is steady and entry points are still accessible. Run the numbers with a lender — the answer usually surprises people.</p>

<h2 id="faq">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>What is the average home price in Englewood Colorado in 2026?</h3>

<p>Englewood's median home price in early 2026 sits in the low-to-mid $400Ks for single-family homes — well below the broader Denver Metro median. Condos and townhomes in Englewood start even lower, often in the low $300Ks, making it one of the most accessible suburbs for first-time buyers using FHA or conventional financing.</p>

<h3>Is Englewood Colorado a good place for first-time home buyers?</h3>

<p>Englewood is one of the strongest entry points in the South Denver Metro for first-time buyers. The combination of below-metro-average pricing, RTD light rail access, walkable commercial districts along South Broadway and Old Hampden, and proximity to downtown Denver makes Englewood a practical choice for buyers who want urban convenience without a Denver price tag.</p>

<h3>Can I buy a home in Englewood with an FHA loan?</h3>

<p>Yes — many Englewood homes fall within FHA loan limits for Arapahoe County, which allows for a 3.5% down payment. Condos need to be on the FHA-approved list, so check with your lender. Single-family homes in Englewood are generally FHA-eligible as long as they meet the property condition requirements at appraisal.</p>
