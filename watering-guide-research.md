## District-by-District Restrictions

### Denver Water (serves Littleton, Centennial, Lone Tree)
- **Stage 1 drought** — mandatory 2 days/week
- Even addresses: Sunday + Thursday
- Odd addresses: Wednesday + Saturday
- Commercial/multifamily: Tuesday + Friday
- No watering 10am–6pm
- Don't turn on sprinklers until mid-to-late May
- New lawn installations NOT allowed
- Goal: 20% reduction
- Source: https://www.denverwater.org/residential/rebates-and-conservation-tips/summer-watering-rules

### Highlands Ranch Metro District
- **Stage 1 drought restrictions** enacted
- Odd addresses: Wednesday + Saturday
- Even addresses: Thursday + Sunday
- Multi-family/apartments: Monday + Friday
- Non-residential: Tuesday + Sunday
- No watering 10am–6pm (May 1 – Sept 30)
- Snowpack at 38%, demand 23% above predicted
- Goal: 15–20% reduction
- Source: https://www.douglascountynewspress.com/news/article_20de4eb0-8819-44fa-8c59-c31350d26d4c.html

### Parker Water & Sanitation District
- No watering 10am–6pm
- Water waste prohibited (runoff, overspray, leaks)
- Free sprinkler consultations via Resource Central partnership
- Contact: conservation@pwsd.org
- Source: https://pwsd.org/3366/Watering-Restrictions

### Castle Rock Water
- **No drought restrictions yet** — but considering Stage 1 (voluntary 10% cut, requires Town Council approval)
- Mandatory year-round schedule: every 3rd day based on address symbol (square/circle/diamond)
- Watering allowed before 8am or after 8pm
- Season: May 1 – Sept 30
- Reduced water budgets for 2026 — exceed and pay surcharges ($6.91/1000 gal, rising to $10.31)
- Reservoir storage at record high despite low snowpack (Reservoir 2 came online)
- Source: https://crconserve.com/158/Watering-Schedule

### Castle Pines North Metropolitan District
- Address-based schedule (last digit of address determines watering day)
- Specific 2026 schedule not yet published at time of research
- Source: https://www.cpnmd.org/water

### Englewood Water District
- **Modified Phase III Water Shortage** — effective Feb 8, 2026 through July 1, 2026
- ONE watering day per week based on last digit of address
- Sprinklers: 12:01am–4am or 8pm–11:59pm
- Low-volume (hand watering, micro-irrigation): before 8am or after 6pm
- This is the most restrictive district in Jacob's coverage area
- Source: https://englewoodwater.com/water-restrictions/

## Smart Watering Tips (for the "how to water well" angle)

### Deep & Infrequent Watering
- Wet soil to 3–5 inches per irrigation
- 2 days/week with deep watering grows deeper roots
- Frequent short watering = shallow roots, disease-prone turf

### Water Requirements
- Colorado lawns need 1–1.5 inches/week in summer
- Kentucky bluegrass (95% of CO lawns): 1" in April/May, 1.25" in June, 1.5" in July
- Hot/dry/windy conditions: up to 2.25"/week

### Cycle & Soak Method
- Divide total watering time per zone by 3
- Water in short bursts with rest periods between
- Prevents runoff, allows deeper soil penetration

### Best Timing
- Early morning (before 10am) or late evening (after 6pm)
- Morning preferred to reduce disease risk

### Mowing
- Keep at 2.5–3 inches for all Colorado species
- Taller grass retains more moisture
