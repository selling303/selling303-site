The Challenge

Six People. One Starter Home. A Market That Wasn't Making It Easy.

The family had outgrown their Centennial home in every way that matters — bedrooms, bathrooms, backyard. With four kids and two adults sharing a space that was never designed for six, they needed to move. Not someday. Now.

The problem? The South Denver Metro was deep in a seller's market. Every home they wanted to buy was attracting multiple offers the weekend it hit the MLS. The idea of selling their current home, navigating a bidding war on the next one, and somehow timing it all without living in a hotel felt overwhelming.

They needed someone who could manage both sides of the move, keep them informed without drowning them in details, and execute fast when the right opportunity appeared.

The Plan

First, Find Them a Home. Then, Sell the One They Had.

Before the yard sign went up, Jacob went to work on the buy side. He reached out to agents in the neighborhoods that fit the family's criteria and identified a property that hadn't hit the open market yet. By negotiating directly with the seller, he helped the family go under contract off-market — no bidding war, no weekend of anxiety, no escalation clause.

With a new home secured, the pressure was off. Jacob then prepared a detailed plan for selling the Birch Street home: professional photography, strategic pricing designed to attract multiple buyers, and a launch timed to maximize first-weekend traffic.

Every step was communicated clearly. The family knew what to expect, when to expect it, and exactly what was happening with their transaction in real time.

The Result

$82,000 Over Asking. Closed in Four Days.

The Birch Street home attracted strong buyer interest from the first day on market. Within four days, the family had an accepted offer at $707,000 — $82,000 above the list price, a 113.1% sale-to-list ratio that stands among the strongest results in the Centennial market that year.

The family moved into a home that finally had room for everyone. The transition was seamless, the timeline worked, and they walked away with far more equity than they expected.
